General idea and terminology

The ReMeta toolbox allows researchers to estimate latent parameters of observers that perform a decision-making task involving confidence ratings. Type 1 parameters refer to parameters that describe the decision-making process, while Type 2 parameters refer to parameters that describe the confidence rating process.

The focus of Remeta is an estimate of metacognitive noise, i.e. how accurately observers can monitor their own performance, and metacognitive bias, i.e. whether observers tend to be over- or under-confident.

The basic architecture of the toolbox is shown in the figure below.

From stimulus xxx to decisions DDD...

Based on a stimulus input xxx, the observer computes a decision value yyy, the sign of which determines a type 1 decision DDD. This process can be characterized by a variety of type 1 parameters, including type 1 noise σ1\sigma_{1}σ1​ (sensory or decisional noise) and a type 1 decision bias δ1\delta_1δ1​.

... from type 1 decision values yyy to confidence ratings CCC

The absolute value of the noisy decision value — labeled type 1 evidence z1z_1z1​ — is the relevant input to the type 2 level. It may or may not be subject to a multiplicative bias φ2\varphi_2φ2​, resulting in type 2 evidence z2z_2z2​.

By design, the model assumes that observers represent confidence as probability correct, thus computing confidence as c=p(correct∣z2,σ^1)c=p(\text{correct}\mid z_2,\hat{\sigma}_1)c=p(correct∣z2​,σ^1​). This is sometimes referred to as Bayesian confidence1.

Finally, the mapping from an internal estimate of confidence ccc to reported confidence CCC is governed by a set of confidence criteria γ1,…,γn\gamma^1, \dots, \gamma^nγ1,…,γn.

Noise at the type 2 stage

ReMeta considers three possible sources of metacognitive noise. First, the “readout” of type 1 evidence may not be perfect, which is referred to as a noisy-readout model2.

Second, for the computation of p(correct∣z2,σ^1)p(\text{correct}\mid z_2,\hat{\sigma}_1)p(correct∣z2​,σ^1​), the observer invariably needs an estimate σ^1\hat{\sigma}_1σ^1​ of their own type 1 noise σ1\sigma_1σ1​. This is far from trivial and may thus plausibly be subject to noise itself3. This is referred to as noisy-temperature model.

Finally, internal representations of confidence need to be communicated to the experimenter. This too is plausibly a lossy process and may inject noise into the process. This last form of metacognitive noise is referred to as a noisy-report model4.

In the figure above, all three possible sources of noise are indicated with the same parameter symbol σ2\sigma_2σ2​. Not because, noise is thought to be identical, but because in ReMeta, the researcher must decide for one metacognitive noise source and hence only a single metacognitive noise parameter σ2\sigma_2σ2​ will be estimated.
